PROLOGUE

Founded in 2013, Bellabeat is a manufacturer of smart products focused on women's health. Founder Urka Sren and Sando Mur started manufacturing Bellabeat products with hi-tech technology to inform and inspire women around the world. By 2016 Bellabeat expanded its operations around the world and with an increased customer base Bellabeat introduced few more products revolving around women's health.

To grow and bring change to more women around the globe, the founders of Bellabeat want to analyze current customer data to understand trends and possible recommendations for marketing strategies to improve Bellabeat further.

 

PHASE ONE : ASK

Being a Junior Data Analyst working at the Marketing Analyst Team of Bellabeat, My task is to focus on one of Bellabeats products and analyze smart device data to gain insight into how consumers are using their smart devices. My analysis will then help guide marketing strategy for the company. I will present analysis to the Bellabeat executive team along with your high-level recommendations for Bellabeats marketing strategy.

 

Task in hand: To understand trends in smart device usage through data available for non Bellabeat customers and compare it with Bellabeat. How could these trends apply to Bellabeat customers? Also to understand how these trends could help influence Bellabeat marketing strategy.

 

PHASE TWO : PREPARE

 

Based on initial discussion with Stakeholders, I was recommended to use FitBit Fitness Tracker Data. FitBit Fitness Tracker Data (CC0: Public Domain, dataset made available through Mobius): This Kaggle data set contains personal fitness tracker from thirty fitbit users. Thirty eligible Fitbit users consented to the submission of personal tracker data, including minute-level output for physical activity, heart rate, and sleep monitoring. It includes information about daily activity, steps, and heart rate that can be used to explore users habits. Few Points about data in general are as follows:

 

Where is the Data?

The data used for this analysis is from a public dataset on kaggle https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/arashnic/fitbit

How was the Data collected?

The data is collected from 30 users of the app with their consent. The period from which data is collected is between April 2016-May 2016. Collected data includes information regarding steps taken, activity level, calories burned, sleep quality, and weight.

How credible is the data?

I checked the data and tested on ROCCC parameters. Here are the findings.

 

Reliable - Data is unreliable as its collected from only 30 people so even though it's collected from the app directly, due to very small sample size its insufficient to rely on this data only. It can help in understanding trends upto certain extent.

Original - Data is not original as its not collected by a first party.

Comprehensive - The data sets are comprehensive.

Current - No, the data sets are not current. The data sets are from 2016.

Cited - The data sets are collected, distributed, and cited by Amazon Mechanical Turk.

Data Integrity - Data is collected during Apr.2016 - May 2016 and sample size was only 30 users. That makes data really small and that too restricted to only specific periods, not representing different months or seasons. In short accuracy, completeness, consistency and trustworthiness remain unchecked.

Data Privacy, Security and Accessibility - As mentioned data used is public data set and available to the public so easily accessible and no privacy at all.

Other Limitations - Data lacks in one major department i.e. gender. Bellabeat is conducting this analysis for their smart devices for women so working on data with no gender information is nothing short of bad data. Another drawback is no mention of geographical areas covered in data. Metadata is also not really giving any information.
